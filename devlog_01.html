So I'm Making an Idle RPG
Vaehan's Fantasy Idle RPG — Design Journal
March 2026 Godot 4 Design Phase Solo Dev

Never in a million years would I have thought I'd be creating a game, much less writing a dev log about it. But honestly, I think this has been a long time coming. I've loved video games since the NES days. I remember spending hours lost in Final Fantasy I, just soaking in the world that had been built. Fast forward to high school, where one of my closest friends and I used to joke about starting our own game company "when we grow up." It was a fun idea, but we didn't exactly have the means (or the education yet) to make it happen. Many years later in college, I came across Disgaea and my mind was completely blown. Here was a game that celebrated the "numbers go up" philosophy and never once took itself too seriously. I've been quietly wanting to make something that mixed those two worlds ever since, but life had other plans.

Fast forward to today: the idea is still there (along with a few others), and I've finally decided to just give game development a try. So here we are!

So What's the Game?

It's a high fantasy idle RPG. You pick a class, your hero goes out and fights enemies automatically, earns XP and loot, and gets stronger over time. All combat is fully idle with no clicking required once you've made your setup decisions. The game runs whether you're watching or not. When you do pop back in, you can return to town, fix the place up, do some crafting, and head back out.

The design philosophy I keep coming back to is: never punish absence. This should feel like a companion game, something running on a second monitor while you work or watch something else. If you step away for an extended period, coming back should feel like a reward, not a pile of things you missed. Progress always accumulates and there's no wrong path. The game should continuously push you forward.

One thing that sets this apart from a typical idle game: there's a full story here, with a beginning, a middle, and an end. I expect there to be some kind of rebirth system (still working through the details on that one), but the experience will be a little different from a true incremental game.

"This game is going to bend some genres and be a love letter to gaming in the 80s and 90s."
The Four Classes

There are four base classes and you can switch between them freely. Each one has its own level track and its own combat identity. I want switching class to feel like more than just a stat swap. More on how deep that goes in a future log, but here's the short version:

⚔ Warrior
High damage, high HP. Lands critical hits and staggers enemies, interrupting their attacks mid-swing.
Trait: Battle-Hardened
✦ Priest
Sustain-focused. Each ability runs its own pulse timer, layered rhythms that keep the hero alive through attrition.
Trait: Congregation
✧ Mage
High burst, lower HP. Hits chain into magic sequences that apply debuffs the deeper they go. Volatile but powerful.
Trait: Glass Cannon
◈ Thief
Fast and evasive. Dodges attacks entirely and punishes enemies with immediate counter strikes.
Trait: Read the Room
A Quick Word on How I'm Using AI

I want to be upfront about this game from the start. I'm using Claude to help me organize ideas, stress-test design decisions and help me craft messages. I've found Claude is great for catching long-term problems I might not be thinking about when I'm excited about a new sub-system I created. I'm also going to lean on it pretty heavily for coding. I can write simple programs but nothing on this scale, so AI will help me build systems I can actually understand and tweak as necessary.

What I will not be using AI for is creativity. The core of this game comes from my own ideas, and I'll be partnering with artists and composers to bring the art and music to life. I truly believe AI is a powerful tool, but it can never replace human creativity.

✦ My Goals for This Project

1. Create an interesting idle game with my own ideas that keeps you coming back.
2. Deepen my understanding of AI and how it can enhance human-driven ideas.
3. Keep me out of trouble.

Where Things Stand

Right now I'm deep in the design phase. I have a full Game Design Document that I've been building out: class systems, combat mechanics, the town and building system, economy, progression. The bones are solid. There's still a lot to figure out (enemy areas, the full item system, ability lists per class) but the design is coming together in a way I'm actually excited about.

I'll be honest, I have no timeline. This is a solo project built around a day job, and I'm not going to make promises I can't keep. But I'll keep writing these logs as things develop. Next one I'll get into the game itself in more detail: why I think you should play it, and what makes it different from the other idle games out there.

Thanks for reading. More soon!
